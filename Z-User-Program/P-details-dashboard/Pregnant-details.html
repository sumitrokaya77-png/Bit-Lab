Pregnancy Resources & Guidelines

Time to Get Pregnant (Conception Planning)

Tracking the Window: Your highest chance of natural conception falls within the 6-day fertile window, concluding exactly on ovulation day.

Basal Body Temperature: Monitor slight thermal baseline shifts first thing in the morning to confirm your precise ovulation release patterns.

Pre-Conception Health: Begin taking 400mcg of folic acid daily at least one to three months prior to active conception cycles to support vital neural development.

What to Do During Pregnancy

First Steps Post-Test: Schedule an initial prenatal diagnostic assessment with an OBGYN between gestational weeks 8 and 10.

Nutritional Alignment: Focus on iron-rich leafy greens, proteins, and pasteurized dairy, while scaling up fluid intake to roughly 2.5–3 liters per day.

Safe Physical Activity: Engage in low-impact cardio, prenatal yoga, or core floor work to build core stamina and pelvic floor mobility.

Things to Strictly Avoid

Dietary Restrictions: Skip raw/unpasteurized seafood, deli meats susceptible to listeria, raw eggs, and excess daily caffeine intake.

Environmental Risks: Restrict hot tub configurations, saunas, and intense contact activities that threaten to unsafely raise your core body temperature.
